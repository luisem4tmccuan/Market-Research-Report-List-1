<p><p>The utility poles market comprises various types, each suited for specific applications. Wood poles are traditional, cost-effective, and widely used but have limited lifespan and resistance to weather. Concrete poles offer durability and resistance to rot, making them ideal for long-term use but are heavier and more expensive. Steel poles provide strength and longevity but can be prone to corrosion without proper treatment. Fiber-reinforced composite (FRC) poles are lightweight, corrosion-resistant, and environmentally friendly, emerging as a modern alternative in the market.</p></p>

<p><p>Utility poles serve as critical infrastructure for both power lines and subtransmission lines, facilitating the distribution of electricity from power plants to consumers. In power lines, tall poles support high-voltage transmission cables, ensuring efficient long-distance electricity transfer. Subtransmission lines utilize shorter utility poles to convey power from substations to local distribution networks, enhancing grid reliability. Both applications are essential for maintaining energy supply, managing load distribution, and ensuring safety in urban and rural environments, ultimately supporting energy accessibility.</p></p>
