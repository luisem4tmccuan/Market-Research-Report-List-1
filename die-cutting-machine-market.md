<p><ul><li>Metal-to-Metal</li><li>Perfing</li><li>Slitting</li><li>Kiss Cutting</li><li>Scoring</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The die cutting machine market encompasses various techniques tailored for different applications. Metal-to-metal die cutting involves creating precise cuts through two metal sheets, ideal for manufacturing parts. Perfing produces a series of perforations in materials for easy tearing. Slitting divides large rolls of material into narrower widths. Kiss cutting cuts through the top layer while leaving the backing intact, commonly used for stickers. Scoring creates a crease for easy folding, widely used in packaging and card manufacturing.</p></p>
