<p><p>The microbial agricultural inoculants market encompasses two primary types: soil inoculation and seed inoculation. Soil inoculation involves the application of beneficial microbes directly to the soil to enhance nutrient availability, improve soil health, and promote plant growth. In contrast, seed inoculation entails treating seeds with specific microorganisms before planting, which helps establish a beneficial relationship between the microbes and emerging plants, boosting germination rates and early plant development. Both methods aim to increase agricultural productivity sustainably.</p></p>
