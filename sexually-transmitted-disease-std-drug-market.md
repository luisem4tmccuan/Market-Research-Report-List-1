<p><p>The STD drug market comprises three key types: antibiotics, antivirals, and vaccines. Antibiotics are primarily used to treat bacterial STDs like chlamydia and gonorrhea, effectively eliminating infections. Antivirals target viral STDs such as herpes and HIV, managing symptoms and viral load but not curing the infection. Vaccines, such as those for HPV, provide preventative measures against certain STDs, reducing infection risk. Together, these categories form a comprehensive approach to managing and preventing sexually transmitted diseases.</p></p>
